# Multi-product Hamiltonian Simulation with Explicit Commutator Scaling

**arXiv:** [2403.08922](https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.08922)

**Authors:** Junaid Aftab, Dong An, Konstantina Trivisa

**Submission Date:** March 13, 2024

---

## Abstract

Rigorous analysis of multi-product formula (MPF) algorithms demonstrating explicit commutator scaling with near-optimal time and precision dependence.

---

## Key Results

### Main Error Bound
The multi-product formula error scales as:
$$\epsilon \sim \mathcal{O}\left(\sum_{\text{nested commutators}} \|[H_j, [H_k, \ldots]]\| \cdot t^{p+1}\right)$$

### Performance Advantages
- **Polynomial speedup:** In both system size and evolution time vs higher-order Trotter
- **Exponential precision improvement:** Compared to standard product formulas
- **vs Post-Trotter methods:** Polynomially better system-size scaling with only polylog overhead in time and precision

### Complexity
MPF achieves:
- Commutator scaling (like Trotter)
- Near-optimal polylog(1/Îµ) precision dependence (like LCU/QSP)
